How I think the world's problems can feasibly be solved on a global scale (credit to statistician Joel Cohen)

  • imagine what we could accomplish if we added billions of educated, fed, and nurtured people to our arsenal of problem solvers.
  • education - Everyone in the world should have access to a good primary and secondary education. Women who are educated have less children on average, because it provides them with a means of achieving status other than childbearing. People should be educated about diversity of values, i.e. they should recognize that other people have different values than them, and they should be educated about how their body works and what is necessary for health.
  • nutrition - 1 billion people go to bed hungry each night, and we have the food to feed them.
  • contraception - Around half of the children born around the world are unplanned or unwanted. Universal access to contraception will help ensure that every child is a wanted child.
